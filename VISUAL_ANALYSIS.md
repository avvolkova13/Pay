# Visual Analysis: Sui Reference

This document is a permanent visual reference for PAYWAYS development. It captures what to learn from Sui without copying its brand, graphics, wording, Web3 identity or product behavior.

## Visual Principles

- Build a calm, premium, international technology presence.
- Use light surfaces, strong hierarchy and large amounts of negative space.
- Make each section feel designed for its content, not assembled from repeated generic blocks.
- Let interface-like visuals explain product logic: payment flows, review states, integration layers, operational dashboards.
- Keep decorative elements purposeful and tied to infrastructure, routing, trust or onboarding.

## Motion

- Motion should clarify sequence and state: reveal, route, verify, connect, launch.
- Prefer subtle scroll reveals, line movement, panel transitions and precise hover feedback.
- Avoid excessive animation, random glowing movement, heavy parallax or motion that distracts from fintech trust.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Start with CSS and small React interactions before adding a motion library.

## Composition

- Use scroll storytelling: hero establishes promise, later sections unfold services, API, industries, trust, commercial logic and onboarding.
- Vary section structure: asymmetric maps, split workspaces, pipeline layouts, matrices and focused panels.
- Avoid repeating the same centered heading plus equal card grid pattern.
- Leave enough breathing room between sections so the page feels enterprise-grade, not cramped.

## Typography

- Use confident display typography in the hero and major sections.
- Keep supporting text measured, readable and calm.
- Separate interface labels, body copy, legal text and CTA labels with clear scale and weight.
- Avoid tiny SaaS-style text everywhere or overusing uppercase labels.

## Color System

- Use a light base: white, ice blue, pale cyan and blue-gray neutrals.
- Use blue as the trust and navigation anchor: CTAs, active states, important paths and infrastructure lines.
- Gradients should create depth or hierarchy, not decoration for its own sake.
- Avoid crypto-style neon, purple-blue clichés, random glow blobs and noisy color effects.

## Cards And Panels

- Cards should represent meaningful modules, not filler.
- Avoid identical card grids in every section.
- Use cards selectively for services, forms, legal blocks and dashboard-like surfaces.
- Mix cards with tables, pipelines, consoles, line systems and open layouts.
- Keep radii controlled and avoid nesting cards inside cards.

## Storytelling

- The page should move from confidence to capability:
  1. What PAYWAYS does.
  2. Which payment services it supports.
  3. How integration can work.
  4. Which industries fit.
  5. How trust and review are handled.
  6. How commercial terms are formed.
  7. How a business starts.
  8. How the demo checkout behaves.
- Every section should answer a business question, not just decorate the page.

## Use

- Light blue premium fintech atmosphere.
- Large, high-confidence hero.
- Payment-flow and API-console motifs.
- Line systems, grids, review states and operational surfaces.
- Smooth hover states and restrained scroll reveals.
- Clear demo boundaries for login and checkout.

## Avoid

- Copying Sui brand assets, logo, text, graphics, Web3 logic or product claims.
- Crypto aesthetics.
- Stock-like illustrations or generated images.
- Fake certificates, fake tariffs or unsupported business claims.
- Generic SaaS card grids.
- Random gradients, bokeh, orbs and glow blobs.
- Overbuilt motion that hurts trust or performance.

## Recommended Technologies

- Next.js App Router with static export.
- TypeScript.
- CSS-first layout and motion.
- Small React client components for forms, tabs, menu and modal states.
- No new motion library unless CSS/React is insufficient and the dependency is approved first.
- Browser QA for routes, forms, language switch, responsive sizes and overflow.
